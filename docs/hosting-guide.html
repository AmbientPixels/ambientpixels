FAQ: Hosting, Domains & DNS

What's a domain name?

A domain is your website's address — like yourname.com. You register it through services like Namecheap, GoDaddy, or Google Domains.

What's web hosting?

Hosting is the computer space where your website lives. It delivers your files (HTML, images, etc.) to anyone visiting your domain.

What's DNS?

DNS (Domain Name System) maps your domain name to your hosting location. Think of it like a GPS that tells browsers where to find your website.

Can I buy a domain and host somewhere else?

Yes — and many do. You can buy a domain from one company and point it to another platform for hosting.

Do I need coding skills?

Level 1: No code required
Level 2: Minimal — some file handling or Git basics
Level 3: Coding or developer assistance recommended

Do I need HTTPS/SSL?

Absolutely — and most platforms include it for free:

  • Wix, GoDaddy: enabled by default
  • Netlify, Vercel, Cloudflare: automatic HTTPS
  • DIY? You'll configure it via your domain or CDN provider
