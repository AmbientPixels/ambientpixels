Microsoft Casual Games – Support Migration

Producer role overseeing transition from Zendesk to Xbox Support for a family of games

Project Overview

As a content producer at Xbox, I led the support migration for Microsoft Casual Games, managing the transition from the Zendesk help center to the Xbox Support platform. My dual-role involved maintaining and updating content on both platforms during the transition period, ensuring a seamless experience for users as we moved to the new support.xbox.com infrastructure.

Working closely with PMs and backend developers, I coordinated the content migration while continuing to support the existing Zendesk platform until the transition was complete. This included managing parallel content updates, implementing redirects, and training support teams on the new platform while maintaining the legacy system.

After the migration, I continued supporting the new Xbox Support platform, handling page builds, feedback form management, and coordinating content updates across partner teams. My role was crucial in ensuring zero disruption to users throughout the transition period.

Migration in Motion

Rebuilding from the inside out—hands-on with systems, structure, and support.

During the migration from Zendesk to Xbox Support, I worked deeply in both platforms—maintaining legacy content while constructing the new support experience. I coordinated updates, prepped content for localization, and managed the structured transition of forms, articles, and metadata across platforms.

From accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA) to visual QA, device testing, and link validation, I helped ensure every asset, interaction, and content element was polished and production-ready. This direct work enabled a smooth launch of the new support site—adapted for a global, multilingual audience.
